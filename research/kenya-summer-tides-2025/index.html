Your 9-Night Festival Adventure

The ultimate Summer Tides experience: beach days, festival nights, whale sharks & recovery

29
JUN
✈️ Arrive in Malindi
Fly into Malindi Airport (direct from Nairobi, 1hr) or Mombasa + transfer. Check into your accommodation. Beach walk to scout the scene. Dinner with the crew. Early night - you'll need the sleep bank.
30
JUN
⛵ Dhow Cruise & Malindi Heritage
Morning: Explore Malindi Old Town - Vasco da Gama Pillar, Portuguese church, Juma Mosque, local spice markets. Afternoon: Traditional dhow sunset cruise with fresh seafood and Swahili drinks. The perfect coastal Kenya introduction.
1
JUL
🦈 Watamu Marine Adventure
6 AM departure for whale shark snorkeling (July = 90%+ encounter rate!). Swim with the gentle giants. Stop at Watamu Marine Park for coral reef snorkeling. Visit Local Ocean Trust turtle sanctuary. Back by 3pm for pool and rest before festival begins!
2
JUL
🎵 FESTIVAL DAY 1 - Opening Night
Sleep in. Beach & pool. THE NAP (mandatory, 4-7pm). Get ready with the crew. Head to Lost Beach Club. Opening night sets. Pace yourself - it's a marathon!
3
JUL
🌅 FESTIVAL DAY 2 - Main Event
Late breakfast. Pool recovery. Hydrate! THE NAP (non-negotiable). Main headliners tonight. This is THE night. Chase the legendary sunrise set as the sun rises over the Indian Ocean.
4
JUL
🎪 FESTIVAL DAY 3 - Grand Finale
Final day energy. Closing sets. The emotional farewell. Last dance on the beach. After-party until sunrise. Leave it all on the dance floor. 💃🕺
5
JUL
😴 Recovery Day 1
Sleep until whenever. Late breakfast (staff will hold it). Pool. Maybe a massage. Reflect on the memories. Early dinner. Actual full night's sleep.
6
JUL
🏛️ Gede Ruins & Beach Recovery
Morning visit to Gede Ruins - mysterious 12th-century Swahili city lost in the forest. Spot rare golden-rumped elephant shrews. Afternoon spa & beach. Farewell Swahili seafood dinner under the stars.
7
JUL
✈️ Departure
Late checkout. Final swim in the Indian Ocean. Transfer to airport. Fly home with festival memories AND stories of whale sharks, ancient ruins, and Swahili sunsets.
