Bioluminescent Night Dive

📍 Turtle Bay, Watamu

Dive into darkness as the ocean lights up with bioluminescent plankton. Only possible during new moon phases. The glowing effect is otherworldly - every movement creates trails of light. Advanced certification required. Book through Watamu dive centers.

🌙 New moon only
🤿 Advanced cert required
💰 $85/person

💡 Insider Tips

What the locals know

🎵 Festival Transport
Arrange transport BEFORE you go. Ask your hotel to book a taxi for all festival nights. Agree on price upfront (~$15-20 each way). Uber doesn't work well here.
🦈 Whale Shark Timing
Go out at first light (6 AM). By 9 AM, there can be 10+ boats around each shark. Early boats often get 30+ minutes of solo time with the giants.
💰 Cash is King
Bring KES cash for local experiences. ATMs exist but can be unreliable. Festival vendors prefer M-Pesa (Kenya's mobile money) - ask a local to help you set it up.
🌊 Tide Check
Check tides before beach activities. Low tide exposes rock pools for exploring, but swimming is better at high tide. Apps like "Tide Charts" work well here.
🧥 Festival Layers
The sea breeze gets COLD at 4-6 AM during sunrise sets. Bring a light jacket or hoodie. You'll thank us when everyone else is shivering.
📿 Respect Local Culture
The coast is predominantly Muslim. Dress modestly when visiting Malindi Old Town or markets. Festival beach = anything goes. Town = cover up.
